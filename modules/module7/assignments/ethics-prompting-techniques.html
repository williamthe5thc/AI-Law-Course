AI Ethics & Advanced Prompting Techniques

    Ethics & Advanced Prompting Assignment 

Deliverables

Submit a comprehensive report containing:

  1. Your prompting strategy design document (7.1)
  2. Documentation of all prompts used, the resulting LLM responses, and your prompt iterations (7.2)
  3. Evaluation of the effectiveness of different prompting techniques (7.3)
  4. Your summary, solution, and reflection on the ethical implications of your findings
    1. Clearly summarize each of the ethical issues
    2. Present a solution to the issue
    3. Reflect on the future implications or problems if this is not addressed
  5. Appendix containing your reference materials for RAG

Assessment Criteria

Your assignment will be assessed based on:

  • Prompting Technique Mastery (30%): Demonstrated skill in designing effective prompts using RAG, CoT, and role-based approaches
  • Ethical Analysis Depth (25%): Thorough exploration of ethical challenges and solutions
  • Critical Evaluation (25%): Insightful assessment of prompting effectiveness and limitations
  • Reflection Quality (20%): Thoughtful consideration of the broader implications of your findings

Resources

  • Recommended LLMs: Claude, GPT-4, Llama 3, or other advanced models with sufficient context windows
  • RAG Resources: LangChain documentation, relevant academic papers on AI ethics
  • CoT Resources: Wei et al. (2022) "Chain of Thought Prompting Elicits Reasoning in Large Language Models"
  • Role-Based Prompting: Kong et al. (2023) "Agent Prompting: Improving LLM Performance through Role-based Instruction"

Key Concepts

  • Retrieval-Augmented Generation: Using external knowledge sources to ground AI responses
  • Chain-of-Thought Reasoning: Breaking down complex reasoning into sequential steps
  • Role-Based Prompting: Directing AI to adopt specific professional perspectives
  • Ethical Analysis: Systematic examination of ethical implications in AI implementation
  • Iterative Improvement: Progressive refinement of prompting techniques

Tips for Success

  • Document everything - Keep detailed records of all your prompts and responses
  • Be methodical - Systematically compare different techniques for the same ethical issue
  • Choose quality sources - Select authoritative, diverse sources for your RAG prompting
  • Include negative results - Document approaches that didn't work and explain why
  • Connect to your profession - Ground your analysis in relevant industry contexts
