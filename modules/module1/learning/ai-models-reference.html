AI Models & Capabilities Reference Sheet

    Learning Activity 3: AI Models Reference 

Review this handy reference guide, which shows different AI models and their capabilities. Think of it as your AI field guide – it breaks down what each type of AI does best, from text generation to image creation. However, as you will soon learn, AI is a RAPIDLY changing field, and this may already be out of date!

Infographic: AI Models and Their Capabilities

Note: In the actual course, an infographic will appear here showing different AI models and their capabilities in a visual format.

Key AI Model Types

  • Large Language Models (LLMs) - Text generation and understanding (e.g., GPT-4, Claude, LLaMA)
  • Text-to-Image Models - Generate images from text descriptions (e.g., DALL-E, Midjourney, Stable Diffusion)
  • Multimodal Models - Process multiple types of data like text and images together (e.g., GPT-4 with vision)
  • Specialized Legal AI - Tools designed specifically for legal research, document review, and contract analysis
  • Voice & Speech Models - Recognize and generate human speech (e.g., Whisper, ElevenLabs)

Using This Reference

  • Keep it handy - Refer to this guide when deciding which AI tool might be most appropriate for a task
  • Recognize limitations - Each model type has specific strengths and weaknesses
  • Stay updated - This field evolves rapidly; capabilities expand with new model releases
  • Consider context - Not all AI models are appropriate for all legal contexts due to privacy and security concerns
